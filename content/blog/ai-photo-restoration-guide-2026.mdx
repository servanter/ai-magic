## What is AI Photo Restoration?

AI photo restoration uses machine learning algorithms to automatically detect and repair damage in old or degraded photographs. Unlike manual restoration which requires hours of painstaking work in Photoshop, AI tools can:

- Remove scratches, dust, and stains
- Repair tears and missing sections
- Restore faded colors and contrast
- Enhance facial details and sharpness
- Colorize black and white photos
- Upscale low-resolution images

The best part? Most modern AI restoration tools require zero technical skills—just upload your photo and let the algorithms work their magic.

## Step-by-Step: Restoring a Severely Damaged Photo

Let's walk through restoring a torn, faded photo from the 1950s using VanceAI Photo Restorer:

### Step 1: Prepare Your Image

- Scan or photograph the original at highest resolution possible
- Aim for at least 300 DPI scan quality
- Save as PNG or JPEG (avoid heavy compression)
- Flatten any curled edges before scanning

### Step 2: Upload to VanceAI

1. Go to VanceAI Photo Restorer website
2. Click "Upload Image"
3. Select your scanned photo
4. Wait for automatic processing (usually 10-30 seconds)

### Step 3: Adjust Restoration Settings

The AI will automatically detect damage, but you can fine-tune:

- **Restoration Strength**: Low (subtle) to High (aggressive)
- **Denoise Level**: Remove grain without losing detail
- **Sharpness**: Enhance clarity of faces and objects
- **Color Correction**: Auto-adjust faded colors

### Step 4: Preview and Download

- Compare before/after with split-screen slider
- Check facial details and important elements
- Download full-resolution restored image
- Keep the original safe!

### Step 5: Optional Post-Processing

For severely damaged photos, you may need additional steps:

- **Crop**: Remove unrepairable edges
- **Brightness/Contrast**: Fine-tune after AI restoration
- **Clone Stamp**: Manually fix remaining artifacts (in Photoshop)
- **Print Test**: Check how it looks printed before framing

## Advanced Techniques for Professional Results

### Combining Multiple AI Tools

Don't limit yourself to one tool. The best workflow often involves:

1. **VanceAI** for damage removal and basic restoration
2. **Remini** for facial enhancement
3. **Topaz Gigapixel AI** for upscaling
4. **Restoration.ai** for colorization

Each tool excels at different tasks—combining them yields superior results.

### Handling Extreme Damage

For photos with missing sections:

- Use **AI inpainting** (e.g., Photoshop Generative Fill)
- Feed the AI contextual information (what should be there)
- Process in multiple passes with different settings
- Consider professional services for irreplaceable photos

### Color Restoration Tips

When restoring color photos:

- Start with black and white restoration first
- Apply colorization as separate step
- Reference similar photos from same era for color accuracy
- Manually adjust skin tones (AI often gets this wrong)

### Preserving Grain and Texture

Sometimes you want to keep the vintage look:

- Use lower denoising settings
- Preserve original grain texture
- Don't over-sharpen (can look artificial)
- Consider "aged" filters after restoration

## Common Problems and Solutions

### Problem: AI Removes Too Much Detail

**Solution:** 
- Use lower restoration strength
- Process in multiple gentle passes
- Try different AI models (some are more aggressive)

### Problem: Faces Look Plastic or Over-Smoothed

**Solution:**
- Use tools specialized in facial preservation (Remini, MyHeritage)
- Add subtle noise/grain back in post-processing
- Blend original and restored versions (50/50 opacity)

### Problem: Colors Don't Look Natural

**Solution:**
- Adjust saturation down (AI often over-saturates)
- Use reference photos from same time period
- Manually correct skin tones and sky colors
- Consider professional colorization services

### Problem: Upscaled Image Looks Blurry

**Solution:**
- Restore first, then upscale (not simultaneously)
- Use specialized upscaling tools (Topaz, Real-ESRGAN)
- Don't exceed 400% enlargement in one step
- Add sharpening as final step
