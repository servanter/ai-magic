## Understanding ID Photo Requirements

ID and passport photos have strict technical requirements that vary by country and document type. Getting these wrong can result in your application being rejected. Here are the most common standards:

### Common Size Requirements

| Document Type | Dimensions | Background |
|---|---|---|
| US Passport | 2×2 inches (51×51mm) | White or off-white |
| UK Passport | 35×45mm | Light grey or cream |
| EU Biometric Passport | 35×45mm | White or light grey |
| Chinese ID / 1-Inch Photo | 25×35mm (1 inch) | White or blue |
| Visa Photos (most countries) | 35×45mm or 2×2 inches | White |
| Driver's License (US) | Varies by state | White or light blue |

### Universal Requirements

Regardless of country, almost all ID photos share these rules:

- **Background**: Plain, solid, light-colored background (white, off-white, or light blue depending on document)
- **Expression**: Neutral, mouth closed, no smiling (some countries allow a slight smile)
- **Glasses**: Most modern passports and IDs require photos without glasses
- **Head position**: Facing directly forward, head centered in frame
- **Head coverage**: No hats, religious headwear may be acceptable with documentation
- **Eyes**: Clearly visible, open, and looking directly at the camera
- **Lighting**: Even, no harsh shadows on the face or background
- **Recency**: Most documents require photos taken within the last 6 months

## Common Mistakes That Get Photos Rejected

Rejection of ID photos is more common than you'd think. The most frequent reasons include:

1. **Incorrect dimensions or aspect ratio**: Many people use phone selfies without cropping to the correct size.
2. **Wrong background color**: A cream or beige background might look "white" on screen but fail automated checks.
3. **Shadows on the background**: Even subtle shadows behind the subject can trigger rejection.
4. **Looking slightly off-center**: Eyes should be level and centered horizontally.
5. **Wearing glasses**: Updated international standards (including ICAO) now generally prohibit glasses in biometric passport photos.
6. **Expression issues**: Smiling too broadly, raised eyebrows, or open mouth.
7. **Hair covering the face**: Ensuring hair doesn't obscure the facial region is critical.
8. **Low resolution**: Digital submissions often require at least 600×600 pixels at 300 DPI.

## Tips for Taking the Perfect ID Photo at Home

Even with AI assistance, starting with a good source photo improves results significantly:

### Lighting
- Stand near a large window with natural, diffused daylight
- Avoid direct sunlight which creates harsh shadows
- Ensure even lighting on both sides of your face — use a white wall or board on the opposite side as a reflector if needed
- Avoid colored light sources that might affect skin tone

### Background (Even If AI Will Replace It)
- Shooting against a plain, light-colored wall gives the AI cleaner data to work with
- Avoid busy backgrounds, strong patterns, or backgrounds that match your clothing or hair color

### Camera and Framing
- Use a tripod or prop your phone against something stable for a non-blurry result
- Frame so your head and shoulders fill the upper two-thirds of the image
- Ensure your entire head (including hair at the top) is clearly in frame
- Take multiple shots — slight variations in expression and angle give you options

### Clothing and Appearance
- Wear something you'd actually wear for official purposes
- Avoid white tops if the required background is white (you'll blend in)
- Ensure hair is tidy and not covering your face
- Remove glasses before shooting
